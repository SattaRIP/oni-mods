# More Clothing v1.2.0 — The Mannequin dresses up

The Mannequin got a complete makeover, footwear finally *shows*, and the
recipes got friendlier. This is the biggest update since release.

## The Mannequin is now an actual mannequin

The old version was a dressform that held a folded garment on a stand. The
new one is a **dupe-shaped display dummy built on the real dupe rig** — the
same skeleton, proportions, and idle pose the game uses for duplicants,
rendered as a faceless linen statue.

That matters because of what it means for the clothes: when you display a
garment, the Mannequin **wears it**. Torso garments drape over its body
exactly as they do on a dupe — same art, same fit. Boots go on its feet.
And its head is real dupe head size, which brings us to…

**Hats.** Select a Mannequin and use *Add Hat / Next Hat / Remove Hat* in
its menu to put any hat in the game on it — mining helmets, chef toques,
astronaut domes, the lot. The choice saves with your colony. Between
garments, boots, and hats, you can finally build the boutique window
display your barracks deserves.

A dressed Mannequin radiates decor for the room, with fancier garments
worth more. It lives in the Furniture menu and unlocks with Textile
Production research.

## Footwear you can actually see

Here's a vanilla fact you may not have noticed: **footwear in ONI has no
worn art at all.** A dupe in Rubber Boots looks exactly like a dupe without
them — the game simply never drew boots on feet.

As of this update, More Clothing draws them. **Snazzy Rubber Boots** show
as tall gold rubber boots and **Shoes** as low black dress shoes with a
gold buckle — on dupes wearing them *and* on Mannequins displaying them.
Your golden-booted plumber corps is now visibly golden-booted.

## Recipe & progression changes

- **Multi-fiber recipes:** every fiber cost now accepts Reed Fiber,
  Feather Fiber (Prehistoric Planet DLC), or Rayon Fiber (Ronivans
  Legacy) interchangeably — pick whichever your colony has.
- **Research homes:** the garments and the Mannequin unlock with
  **Textile Production**; the Soft Suit moved to **Hazard Protection**,
  where hazard gear belongs.
- **Soft Suit recipe** now also takes 1 Rubber Boots and 1 Oxygen Mask —
  it covers head to toe, so it's built from head-to-toe parts. Its
  built-in boots fill the shoes slot while worn.
- **Winter Coat** now adds a touch of room decor while worn.
- Soft Suit helmet arming delay raised to 1.5 s so it stops flinching at
  doorways.

## Fixes

- Displayed garments no longer leave a floating item copy hovering above
  the Mannequin.
- Outfit-system garments (Supply Closet skins) display with their correct
  look instead of placeholder art.
- The Mannequin's blueprint is proper hollow line-art, and the built
  statue matches the blueprint's size.

---

*More Clothing is standalone. The Soft Suit and Snazzy upgrades use Bionic
Booster Pack garments as ingredients; the Winter Coat, Shoes, and
Mannequin are DLC-free.*
